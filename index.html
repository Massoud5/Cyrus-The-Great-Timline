Kourosh (Cyrus) the Great

The founder of "Multinational Civilisation"

A time line of "King of the Four Corners of the World" :

  • 558 - The beginning of the reign of Cyrus in Anshan (south-west of actual Iran) and transfer of the Achaemenid capital to Susa.
  • 550 - Defeat of Ishtvigo, king of Mede, by Cyrus and his subsequent conquest of Hegmatane. Alliance of Mede and Persia.
  • 549-548 - Cyrus conquers Parthia, Jorjan and actual Kavkaz countries.
  • 546 - Cyrus conquers Lydian's capital and defeats Lydian King Croesus.
  • 539 - Cyrus conquers Babylon, the richest city in West Asia. A fall that occurred with the no resistance of the Babylonian army on the banks of the Tigris River.
  • 529 - Cyrus invades the Scythian tribes in northeastern Iran and is killed in battle with the Masagets. He was buried in Pasargad. After him, Cambyses II, son of Cyrus, ascended the throne.
  • 1879 - The Cyrus Cylinder was discovered in Babylon (actual Irak) and was taken to London's British Museum.
  • 1971- The United Nations published the Cyrus Cylinder as the Charter of Human Rights in six official languages and a copy of which is kept at the United Nations Headquarter in New York City.
  • October 29th - Cyrus the Great, Day.
